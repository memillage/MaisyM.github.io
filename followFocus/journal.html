Week I
Initially, I tried using Visual Studio with the Platform.io library and provided code from the makefab's github. After adding the code, I ran it, but unfortunately the board wasn't being programmed. After a few more attempts, it still didn't work so I switched to the Arduino IDE. I again used the given code and DW1000 library from the previously mentioned makerfab github. I tried adding the ports, but they weren't showing up. After switching the cables plugged into the computer, it finaly registered the ports and the boards. I also initially had the incorrect baud rate so the output was incorrect. Once I changed it, the correct outputs displayed, showing the distance between the two UWB devices. I then converted the output from meters to feet. The current outputs are not very accurate and will need to be calabrated through testing.
I bought the incorrect power cables to connect to the 12V source. The ones I got were 5.5 x 5.1 power jacks, but I needed 5.5 x 2.5. In addition, the gears I received to use with the lens do not fit on the motor shaft of the Nema 17. To meditate the issue, I bought ridge flange shaft couplings which I will connect to the gear and place onto the motor shaft. To conceptualize the idea, I created a 3D model with the dimensions of each item as seen in figure 1.
Figure 1: Nema 17 stepper motor with gear
